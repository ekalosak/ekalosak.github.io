How to Bevy when your macbook can't

April 30, 2024

Some personal motivation

What better way to learn more about a language than to write a game in it? It's fun, creative, and complex enough to get you below the surface level language features. Rust is cool, I'm skilling up in it, (unrelated but) Polars alone is enough of a reason for an MLE to get involved. And on the search for a framework, Bevy stood out.
Bevy is, from their homepage, a "refreshingly simple data-driven game engine built in Rust". It uses native structs and has a mental model that is pretty easy to "get dangerous" with. Awesome!

But not so fast. My ancient macbook can't run any of even the simplest examples from Bevy's extensive list. Great sad. After a few months ruminating on this issue, trying other projects here and there to salve the Bevy-shaped ache in my heart, I decided to bash my head against it.
By some miracle (i.e. with lots of blog posts by those more skilled than I) it worked.
